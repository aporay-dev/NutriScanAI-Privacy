Getting Started

How do I scan a nutrition label?
  1. Open NutriScan AI and tap the Scan tab
  2. Tap the camera button
  3. Point your camera at the nutrition facts label
  4. Make sure the label is clearly visible and well-lit
  5. Tap the capture button or select from your photo library
  6. Wait a few seconds for AI analysis
  7. View your results!
What makes a good nutrition label photo?

Pro Tips for Best Results:

  • Good lighting: Natural light or bright indoor lighting works best
  • Flat surface: Place the product on a table for a stable shot
  • Fill the frame: Get close enough so the label fills most of the screen
  • Focus: Make sure the text is sharp and readable
  • No glare: Avoid reflections from glossy packaging
  • Straight angle: Hold your phone parallel to the label (not at an angle)

Troubleshooting

My scan failed or results are inaccurate

Try these solutions:

  • Retake the photo with better lighting
  • Clean your camera lens
  • Get closer to the label (but not too close - keep it in focus)
  • Use a flat surface instead of holding the product
  • Avoid shadows and glare on the label
  • Try a different angle if the label has curves or folds

AI Limitation: Sometimes the AI may misread labels, especially if the image quality is poor. Always verify critical information on the original label, especially for allergens or dietary restrictions.

  5. Free up storage: Delete unused apps or photos if storage is low
  6. Reinstall the app: Delete and download again from App Store

If the problem persists, contact support with details about when the crash occurs.

Tips for Best Results

Photography Tips

  • Daylight works best: Natural window light is ideal
  • Flatten the label: Smooth out wrinkles or curves
  • Hold steady: Use both hands or rest phone on a surface
  • Clean lens: Wipe your camera lens with a soft cloth
  • High contrast: Dark text on light background scans better

Getting Accurate Results

  • Capture the entire label: Make sure all nutrition facts are visible
  • Focus on the "Nutrition Facts" section: Don'\''t include ingredient lists (unless you want them analyzed too)
  • One label at a time: Don'\''t scan multiple labels in one photo
  • Standard formats work best: US FDA Nutrition Facts labels are most accurate
  • Verify critical info: Always double-check allergens and serving sizes
